First Curiosity

Having met at Shakespeare and Company, Joyce and Hemingway had quickly become drinking buddies. Hemingway remembers that Joyce always got into bar fights, leaving it up to him to intervene and calm the situation.
Second Curiosity

He invented a 100-letter word to describe thunder
Lightning and thunder terrified the writer since he was a boy, according to his housekeeper natural phenomena were nothing more than the way in which God manifested his anger towards him. In Finnegans Wake, Joyce mix the French, Italian, Greek and Japanese words for "thunder", creating a 100-letter word to represent divine thunder: Bababadalgharaghtakamminarronnkonnbronntonnerronntuonnthunntrovarrhounawnskawntoohoohoordenenthurnuk.
Third Curiosity

Joyce suffered from an illness that led him to undergo 25 eye operations in his lifetime. For years, due to these vision problems. He was forced to write with a large pencil lying on his stomach on the bed dressed only in a white coat. This strange modality seems to have been linked to his vision problem, so the large crayons helped him see what he was writing and the white coat to reflect the light on the pages.
